= Competitive Intelligence

Competitive intelligence is the process of gathering, analyzing, and using information to gain a competitive advantage. It is a key part of business intelligence and is used to make informed decisions about market trends and customer needs.

Key sources for competitive intelligence in life sciences include 
* patent filings 
* scientific publications
* publicly available information about company drug pipelines 

Integrating insights from these data streams enables organizations to monitor competitor activity, identify emerging trends, and make better-informed strategic decisions.

Together, patent, publication, and pipeline intelligence each offer a unique graph view of the competitive landscape.
